# HotDesk Rail — Market & Implementation Analysis

**Concept:** Decommissioned train cabins refurbished into 4–10 person workspaces, stationed across cities as stationary office pods.

**Date:** March 2025

---

## 1. Availability of Old Train Cars

### UK Market

| Source | Type | Typical Price | Notes |
|--------|------|---------------|-------|
| **eBay / Private Listings** | Unrestored carriages | £1,500 – £5,000 | Often heritage Mark 1/2; condition varies widely |
| **Converted / Part-Restored** | Holiday let / residential | £50,000 – £70,000 | E.g. Stannington Pullman (1978 Mark 3, 75ft) sold 2024 |
| **Heritage Societies & Auctions** | Mark 1, Gresley teak, Southern Railway | £8,000 – £30,000+ | G.W. Railwayana Auctions; Heritage Railway Magazine |
| **Rolling Stock Lessors / Operators** | Class 319, 325, HST, etc. | Scrap / negotiated | Often scrapped; some open to sale (e.g. Royal Mail Class 325) |

### Fleet Displacement (2024–2025)

- **Class 319** (London Northwestern) — scrapped as Class 730 enters
- **Class 325** (Royal Mail) — some scrapped; operator open to selling remainder
- **Class 507** (Merseyside) — scrapped as Class 777 enters
- **Class 150/153/158** (Transport for Wales) — returning to lessors
- **HST Power Cars** — some scrapped; Mark 3 coaches may become available
- **Class 90** — DB Cargo scrapping; locomotives, not coaches

### Carriage Types & Dimensions

| Type | Length | Weight | Usable Floor | Best For |
|------|--------|--------|--------------|----------|
| **Mark 1** | 17–20 m | ~35 tonnes | ~45–55 m² | 4–6 person office |
| **Mark 2** | ~19 m | ~32 tonnes | ~50 m² | 4–6 person office |
| **Mark 3** | 23 m (75 ft) | ~35 tonnes | ~60–70 m² | 6–10 person office |

**Conclusion:** Supply exists but is fragmented. Heritage Mark 1/2 are most accessible; Mark 3 and modern stock come via operators/lessors during fleet replacement. Building relationships with preservation societies, auctions, and rolling stock companies is essential.

## 2. Logistics

### Transport by Road

**Specialist providers:** Allelys (UK leader), others for abnormal loads.

**Methods:**
- Ramp + winch (no crane)
- Low-loader / SPMT
- Crane lift when needed

**Typical costs (indicative):**
- **Short move (30 miles):** ~£10,000–£15,000 (crane, rigging, truck, permits)
- **Medium (100 miles):** £15,000–£25,000
- **Long (200+ miles):** £25,000–£40,000+

**Requirements:**
- Abnormal load permits (AIL)
- Police escort for wide loads
- Route surveys (bridges, narrow roads)
- Site access (crane space, hardstanding)

### Siting

- **Heritage lines:** Many not on main network; road delivery common
- **Urban sites:** Need landowner agreement, planning, services (power, water, drainage)
- **Station platforms:** Possible where Network Rail / TOC allows; complex negotiations

## 5. ROI & Economics

### Revenue Assumptions

**4-person cabin (e.g. Mark 1):**
- Private office rate: £600–£900/desk/month → £2,400–£3,600/month
- Or team room hire: £50–£80/hour × 8 hrs × 20 days ≈ £8,000–£12,800/month (if fully booked)

**6–8 person cabin (e.g. Mark 3):**
- £3,600–£6,000/month (desk model)
- Or £12,000–£20,000/month (room hire, high utilisation)

### Simplified ROI (4-Person Cabin)

| Metric | Conservative | Moderate | Optimistic |
|--------|--------------|----------|------------|
| **CapEx** | £80,000 | £135,000 | £180,000 |
| **Monthly revenue** | £2,000 | £2,800 | £3,500 |
| **Occupancy** | 70% | 80% | 90% |
| **Net revenue** | £1,400 | £2,240 | £3,150 |
| **Payback (months)** | 57 | 60 | 57 |
| **Payback (years)** | **4.8** | **5.0** | **4.8** |

*Assumes 30% opex (utilities, maintenance, site, admin).*

### Scale Effects

- **Portfolio of 5–10 cabins:** Shared ops, marketing, and site management improve margins
- **Coworking operator model:** Multiple cabins on one site; membership + room hire
- **B2B / corporate:** Long-term lets to companies; lower churn, more predictable revenue

### Break-Even

At 75–80% occupancy (typical for coworking), a £135k cabin needs ~£2,700/month net revenue. At £700/desk × 4 desks × 80% = £2,240 — marginal. **Pricing at £800+/desk or strong room-hire mix improves viability.**

## 6. Risks & Considerations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|
| **Supply volatility** | Lock options with preservation societies; engage lessors during fleet replacement |
| **Transport cost** | Site carriage near source; batch moves |
| **Planning** | Early engagement with planners; use permitted development where possible |
| **Land access** | Partner with landowners (stations, business parks, heritage lines) |
| **Competition** | Differentiate on authenticity, design, and experience vs. generic pods |

---

## 7. Summary

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| **Availability** | Moderate — heritage carriages accessible; Mark 3/modern stock via operators |
| **Logistics** | Manageable — specialist hauliers exist; transport often exceeds purchase price |
| **Demand** | Strong — office pod market growing; real carriages offer clear differentiation |
| **CapEx per cabin** | £57k–£210k depending on spec and carriage condition |
| **ROI** | 4–5 year payback feasible at 75–80% occupancy and £700+/desk pricing |
| **Scalability** | Better at 5+ cabins with shared ops; B2B and corporate lets improve unit economics |

**Recommendation:** Pilot with 1–2 carriages (Mark 1 or 2) at a single site. Target £80–100k all-in per cabin, premium pricing (£800+/desk or equivalent room hire), and a partner with land (heritage railway, business park, or station). Validate demand and ops before scaling.
